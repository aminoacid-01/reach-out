Resources & Helplines

At REACH OUT, we understand the importance of having access to reliable support and resources when it comes to mental health. Below, you will find a comprehensive list of helplines and mental health organizations that are dedicated to providing assistance, guidance, and support. Whether you are seeking immediate help or looking for ongoing support, these resources are here to help you navigate your mental health journey. Remember, reaching out for help is a sign of strength. You are not alone, and there are people and organizations ready to support you every step of the way.

Helplines

UK based helplines.

Samaritans

You can contact Samaritans 24 hours a day, 365 days a year.
Call 116 123 (free from any phone), call the Samaritans Welsh Language Line on 0808 164 0123 (7pm-11pm every day), email jo@samaritans.org, or even visit some branches in person.

National Suicide Prevention Helpline UK

Offers a supportive listening service to anyone with thoughts of suicide. You can call the National Suicide Prevention Helpline UK on 0800 689 5652 (6pm to midnight every day).

CALM (Campaign Against Living Miserably)

You can call the CALM on 0800 58 58 58 (5pm to midnight every day) if you're affected by suicide or suicidal thoughts. Or if you prefer not to speak on the phone, you could try the CALM webchat service.

Shout

If you would prefer not to talk but want some mental health support, you could text SHOUT to 85258. Shout offers a confidential 24/7 text service providing support if you are in crisis and need immediate help
