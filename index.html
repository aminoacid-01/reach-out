Reach Out

Because Your Mind Matters

At REACH OUT, our mission is to foster mental health awareness, provide support, and create a compassionate community where every voice is heard and valued. We believe that mental well-being is as essential as physical health, and our goal is to ensure that everyone has access to the resources and information they need to thrive.

OUR GOALS:

  1. Destigmatize Mental Health: We are committed to breaking down the stigma associated with mental health. Through education and open dialogue, we aim to create a world where discussing mental health is normalized and free of judgment.
  2. Provide Resources and Advice: We offer a comprehensive range of resources and practical advice to support your mental health journey. Whether you're looking for tips on managing stress, understaning mental health conditions, or connecting with mental health professionals, our goal is to provide you with the tools you need.
  3. Raise Awareness: Our mission is to increase awareness about the importance of mental health. We strive to educate the community about mental health issues and promote understanding through various initiatives and events.

Managing Your Mental Health:

Some general tips for managing your mental health!

Depression:

  • Don't isolate yourself - Although it may be tempting to withdraw from your loved ones and isolate yourself, it will only make you feel worse. It's helpful to have a support network and people to talk to about how you are feeling.
  • Exercise - There is evidence that shows that regular exercise can help with depression. If it seems a bit daunting, start with walking for 20 minutes everyday
  • Try to keep a routine - people with depression tend to get into poor eating and sleeping habits. Try to get up at your normal time, remember to eat regularly and stick to a routine!
  • Seek Help - Sometimes there's only so much that you can do on your own without professional help, and that's fine! Don't be afraid to get professional help if your symptoms continue or worsen.

Anxiety:

  • Keeping a Diary - This can help you to understand why you are anxious in certain situations, potential triggers, and ways to manage your anxiety.
  • Facing your fears Avoiding situations that could make you anxious could actually do more harm than good. Slowly working towards being able to face situations that typically make you anxious can help to make life easier
  • Relaxation techniques Some people find mindfulness and meditation (including breathing exercises and relaxation) help to calm anxiety and reduce tension by focussing awareness on the present moment.

Stress:

  • Make time for yourself - A lot of people work long hours or have a lot of responsibilities, not leaving a lot of time (or energy) to relax and enjoy their hobbies and interests. Try setting aside a couple of nights a week to just have some ‘me time’.
  • Accept things you can’t change - Many things that stress us out are things that are out of our control. Try to shift your focus towards things that you have control over in your life, rather than focusing on things that you can’t change.
  • Don’t rely on substances - Drinking and smoking may cause temporary relief however they will ultimately make your stress worse in the long term if you rely on them. Try to use more healthy coping methods and your mind and body will thank you in the long run.
